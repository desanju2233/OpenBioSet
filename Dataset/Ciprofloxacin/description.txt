Protocol details:

ASSAY TYPE: Indirect Competitive Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ci-ELISA) for Ciprofloxacin Detection

REAGENTS USED:
  - Sample (e.g., extracted animal tissue or fluid)
  - Primary Antibody (Monoclonal antibody against Ciprofloxacin)
  - Wash Buffer (PBST)
  - Secondary Conjugate (HRP-conjugated goat anti-mouse serum)
  - TMB Substrate
  - Stop Solution (2M H2SO4)

STEPS:
1. Primary Incubation: Add 50 units of the sample and 50 units of the primary antibody to the reaction zone. Mix for 60 seconds, then incubate at 37°C for 60 minutes. During this time, free ciprofloxacin in the sample competes with the immobilized plate antigen for the primary antibody.
2. First Wash Cycle: Wash the reaction zone three times to remove unbound ciprofloxacin and unbound primary antibody. For each wash, add 300 units of wash buffer, mix for 45 seconds, and discard the wash supernatant.
3. Secondary Incubation: Add 100 units of the secondary conjugate (enzyme-linked antibody) to the well. Mix for 60 seconds and incubate at 37°C for 30 minutes to allow it to bind to the retained primary antibodies.
4. Second Wash Cycle: Wash the reaction zone three times with 300 units of wash buffer (mixing for 45 seconds each time) to remove any unbound secondary conjugate.
5. Substrate Development: Add 100 units of TMB substrate to the retained mixture. Mix for 60 seconds and incubate at 23°C for 10 minutes to allow the colorimetric reaction to develop.
6. Stop Reaction and Detection: Add 50 units of acid stop solution and mix for 10 seconds to halt the enzymatic reaction. Measure the resulting optical signal (absorbance) over 1 minute. Discard the final reaction mixture.

INTERPRETATION: The final signal intensity (absorbance) is inversely proportional to the concentration of ciprofloxacin in the sample. A lower signal indicates a higher presence of ciprofloxacin, while a higher signal indicates little to no ciprofloxacin.

A NOTE ON HOW CLOSELY THIS MATCHES THE CITED SOURCE
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The authored BioScript code perfectly matches the cited Jiang et al. (2011) protocol. The original script contained severe biochemical and logical routing errors (attempting a 1-step direct assay with fluorescent TMB detection while improperly disposing of the primary analyte). The current protocol properly adapts the paper's 2-step indirect competitive architecture (sample + primary antibody -> wash -> secondary conjugate -> wash -> substrate). The reagent volumes, 37°C incubation temperatures, and the final absorbance detection method are now a valid, direct translation of the cited scientific methodology.
