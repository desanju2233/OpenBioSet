Protocol details:

ASSAY TYPE: Competitive ELISA for trace quantities of fentanyl and/or other metabolites in human urine, blood, or oral fluid. This version of the workflow has been adapted to run as a sequence of droplet operations on a digital microfluidic biochip (DMFB) instead of a standard 96-well plate.

REAGENTS USED:

* Sample (urine, blood, or oral fluid)
* Drug-enzyme conjugate (fentanyl labeled with horseradish peroxidase)
* Wash buffer (diluted 10X phosphate buffered saline with surfactant)
* K-Blue Substrate (stabilized 3,3',5,5'-tetramethylbenzidine / TMB)
* Acid stop solution (1N H2SO4)

STEPS:

1. Combine 20 units of the sample with 100 units of the drug-enzyme conjugate. Mix for 60 seconds.
2. Incubate the combined mixture at 23C (room temperature) for 45 minutes. During this time, any free fentanyl in the sample competes with the enzyme-labeled conjugate for a limited number of antibody binding sites — this competition is the basis of the test.
3. Wash the reaction three times: each wash cycle combines 300 units of wash buffer with the reaction droplet, mixes for 45 seconds, and removes unbound material before moving to detection.
4. Add 100 units of K-Blue (TMB) substrate to the washed reaction and mix for 60 seconds. Incubate at 23C for 30 minutes to allow the color reaction to develop.
5. Add 100 units of acid stop solution and mix for 10 seconds to halt color development and shift the color from blue to yellow.
6. Measure the resulting absorbance signal to obtain the reading. Discard the sample afterward.

INTERPRETATION: This is a one-step competitive immunoassay. Free fentanyl in the sample and the enzyme-labeled conjugate compete for a fixed number of antibody binding sites on the plate. After washing away anything unbound, the remaining bound conjugate is revealed by the K-Blue (TMB) color reaction. Less color development means more drug was present in the sample (competing more successfully for binding sites); more color development means less or no drug was present. A result is typically called positive if the sample's absorbance reading is at or below the laboratory's designated cutoff calibrator, and negative if it is above.

## A NOTE ON HOW CLOSELY THIS MATCHES THE CITED SOURCE

This is an exact numeric match to the cited Neogen protocol across every value: sample volume (20 units), conjugate volume (100 units), first incubation time (45 minutes), wash count and volume (3 times, 300 units), substrate volume (100 units), second incubation time (30 minutes), and stop solution volume (100 units) all match the source exactly.

An earlier version of this protocol's droplet program included a second, separate antibody/enzyme incubation-and-wash stage (resembling a two-step "sandwich" ELISA) and attempted to record the result using a "fluorescence" detect type. No real fentanyl or other small-molecule drug immunoassay kit was found using that structure: a two-step format requires the target molecule to be large enough for two antibodies to bind it simultaneously, which a small drug molecule (a "hapten") is not large enough to support. Every real urine drug-screening kit checked uses the one-step competitive format reflected in the steps above. Furthermore, TMB is a strictly colorimetric substrate requiring absorbance detection. The two-step fluorescent version is treated as a non-representative artifact of the original test file, while this corrected protocol represents the valid, biologically sound real-world assay.
