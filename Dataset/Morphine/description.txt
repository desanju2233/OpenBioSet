Protocol details:

This is a competitive enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) for screening morphine in urine.

REAGENTS USED:
  - Urine Sample
  - Morphine Enzyme Conjugate
  - Distilled Water (Wash Buffer)
  - TMB Substrate
  - Stop Reagent

STEPS:
1. Sample Preparation: Add 20 units of the urine sample to the reaction zone. 
2. Conjugate Incubation: Add 100 units of morphine enzyme conjugate to the sample. Mix the solution for 60 seconds to allow competitive binding between free morphine in the sample and the conjugate.
3. Primary Incubation: Incubate the mixture at 23°C (room temperature) for 60 minutes to reach equilibrium. 
4. Wash Cycle: Wash the reaction zone six times to remove unbound conjugate. For each wash cycle, add 350 units of distilled water, mix for 45 seconds, and discard the wash supernatant.
5. Substrate Development: Add 100 units of TMB substrate to the retained mixture. Mix for 60 seconds, then incubate at 23°C for 30 minutes to allow the colorimetric reaction to develop.
6. Stop Reaction: Add 100 units of acid stop reagent. Mix with the developed substrate for 60 seconds to halt the enzymatic reaction and change the color from blue to yellow.
7. Detection: Measure the resulting optical signal (absorbance) over 1 minute to obtain the reading. Discard the final reaction mixture.

INTERPRETATION: The final signal intensity (absorbance) is inversely proportional to the concentration of morphine in the urine sample. A lower signal indicates a higher presence of morphine, while a higher signal indicates little to no morphine.
