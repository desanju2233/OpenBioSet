Protocol details: 
Perform cDNA synthesis followed by quantitative PCR (qPCR). First, mix the RNA template, random primers, dNTPs, and RNase-free water to a final volume of 20 uL. Incubate the mixture at 65 degrees C for 5 minutes, then cool to 4 degrees C. Add 20 uL of cDNA synthesis cocktail. Split this reaction into two equal 20 uL aliquots. To the main reaction, add 1 uL of reverse transcriptase and mix. To the control reaction, add 1 uL of water and mix. Incubate both reactions sequentially at 25 degrees C for 10 minutes, 50 degrees C for 50 minutes, and 85 degrees C for 5 minutes. Add 1 uL of RNaseH to each and incubate at 37 degrees C for 20 minutes. For the qPCR phase, prepare a master mix consisting of 2X reaction buffer, forward primers, reverse primers, and water. Dispense 24 uL of this master mix and combine it with 1 uL of the synthesized cDNA. Perform an initial thermal denaturation at 94 degrees C for 3 minutes. Next, repeat the following sequence 35 times: heat at 94 degrees C for 15 seconds, anneal at 53 degrees C for 30 seconds, extend at 72 degrees C for 30 seconds, and perform an optical plate read to detect fluorescence. Finally, conduct a melting curve analysis from 65 to 95 degrees C before disposing of the samples.
