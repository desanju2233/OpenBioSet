Protocol details: Perform a colony PCR for screening assemblies. First, dispense 50 units of water into a lysis well and 150 units of LB antibiotic medium into a copy well. Dispense an E. coli colony and split it into two parts. Mix one part with the water to create the lysis well, and mix the other part with the LB medium to create the copy well. Dispense 10 units of PCR mastermix and mix it into the lysis well to create the PCR reaction. Perform an initial denaturation by heating the PCR reaction at 95 degrees C for 10 minutes. Repeat the following thermal cycle 30 times: heat at 95 degrees C for 30 seconds, anneal at 64 degrees C for 15 seconds, and extend at 72 degrees C for 1 minute. Follow with a final extension at 72 degrees C for 10 minutes. Dispense 2 units of gel loading buffer and mix it with the PCR reaction to create the gel sample. Detect fluorescence on the gel sample. If a band is detected (fluorescence > 0), rescue the clone by dispensing 100 units of TY rich media, mixing it with the copy well to create a rescue culture, and heating it at 37 degrees C for 24 hours before disposal. If no band is detected, dispose of the copy well. Finally, dispose of the gel sample.
