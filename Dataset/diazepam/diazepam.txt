Protocol details:

This is a competitive enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) for screening diazepam in urine.

Sample Preparation: Add 50 units of the urine sample and 1 unit of diazepam-enzyme conjugate to the reaction vessel. Mix the solution for 60 seconds.

Primary Antibody Incubation: Add 100 units of diazepam antibody to the sample mixture. Mix for 60 seconds to allow competitive binding between free diazepam in the sample and the enzyme-labeled conjugate.

Incubate the mixture at 23°C (room temperature) for 30 minutes to allow the reaction to reach equilibrium. Remove and discard the supernatant.

Primary Wash: Wash the reaction zone three times to remove unbound conjugate. For each wash cycle, add 250 units of distilled water and a fresh 1 unit of diazepam-enzyme (capture medium). Mix for 45 seconds, then remove and discard the wash supernatant.

Secondary Conjugate Incubation: Add 150 units of HRP (horseradish peroxidase) conjugate and 1 unit of diazepam-enzyme conjugate. Mix thoroughly, then incubate at 23°C for 15 minutes for signal amplification. Remove and discard the supernatant.

Secondary Wash: Repeat the three-cycle wash step. For each cycle, add 250 units of distilled water and 1 unit of diazepam-enzyme, mix for 45 seconds, and discard the supernatant to remove unbound secondary conjugate.

Substrate Development: Add 100 units of TMB (3,3',5,5'-tetramethylbenzidine) substrate and 1 unit of diazepam-enzyme conjugate. Mix the solution and incubate at 23°C for 15 minutes to allow the HRP-catalyzed colorimetric reaction to develop.

Stop Reaction: Add 100 units of acid stop reagent. Mix with the developed substrate for 60 seconds to halt the enzymatic reaction.

Detection: Measure the resulting signal over 30 minutes to obtain the negative/positive reading. Discard the final reaction mixture.

Adaptation note: The cited Neogen kit is a one-step competitive ELISA for benzodiazepine-class analytes (including diazepam, confirmed via the kit's cross-reactivity table) in urine. The original wet-lab protocol uses a single conjugate incubation (45 min) and a single wash cycle (3x), followed by a single TMB substrate incubation (30 min).

The procedure outlined above diverges from this in two ways consistent with adapting a bench protocol to a Digital Microfluidic Biochip (DMFB): (1) it splits what is conceptually a single antibody/conjugate competition step into two discrete mix+incubate stages (sample+enzyme, then mix+antibody), since microfluidic operations are atomic and sequential rather than continuous; and (2) it introduces a second enzyme-conjugate incubation + wash cycle (using a separate hrp_conjugate reagent and a second detection-amplification round) that is not present in the cited kit. This second stage resembles the conjugate/wash structure of a two-step (indirect) ELISA rather than the one-step competitive format described in the cited source.

As we were unable to locate a published two-step diazepam-specific ELISA that matches this exact structure, the protocol is authored to describe the literal compiled operations, using the cited Neogen kit as the closest available real-world reference for reagent identity, volume conventions, and overall competitive-immunoassay logic (where lower color change correlates with a positive/drug-present result), rather than as a claim of exact step-by-step provenance.
