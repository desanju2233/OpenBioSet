================================================================================
  DMF RIBOZYME CLEAVAGE ASSAY PROTOCOL
  Digital Microfluidics Chips for the Execution and Real-Time Monitoring
  of Multiple Ribozymatic Cleavage Reactions
================================================================================

  Source Link : https://www.shihmicrolab.ca/_files/ugd/f0a828_d4758ae71a674751b0117f1e8194db35.pdf
  Paper DOI   : https://doi.org/10.1021/acsomega.1c00239
  Journal     : ACS Omega, 2021, Vol. 6, pp. 22514-22524

================================================================================
  OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This protocol describes the execution of inducible cis-acting hammerhead
ribozyme (HHR) cleavage reactions on a Digital Microfluidics (DMF) chip,
with real-time fluorescence monitoring. The ribozyme undergoes self-cleavage
upon binding to a short single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) input strand. The
cleaved-off RNA output strand is detected via a toehold-mediated strand
displacement reaction (TMSDR), which displaces a quencher (BHQ-3) from a
Cy5-labeled fluorescent probe, generating a measurable fluorescence signal.
The platform supports up to 8 simultaneous reactions (6 assays + 1 positive
control + 1 negative control) per chip.

================================================================================
  CHIP SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

  Platform           : Digital Microfluidics (DMF), electrowetting-on-dielectric
  Chip 1 (kinetics)  : 6 reservoirs, 8 detection points, 3 mixing zones (4 electrodes each)
  Chip 2 (multi-rxn) : 5 reservoirs, 3 x 17 electrode matrix, 8 detection points
  Electrode spacing  : 60 um interelectrode gap (2x wiring thickness of 30 um)
  Electrode shape    : Skewed-wave edges (prevents dielectric breakdown and droplet stranding)
  Droplet volume     : 0.5 +/- 0.08 uL (dispensed), 2 uL (cleavage buffer, pipetted)
  Actuation          : 178 V RMS, 15 kHz sine wave, 0.7 s per electrode step

================================================================================
  REAGENTS AND MATERIALS
================================================================================

  All general-use chemicals purchased from Sigma-Aldrich (St. Louis, MO)
  unless otherwise stated.

  1. RIBOZYME SOLUTION
     - Type       : Inducible cis-acting hammerhead ribozyme (HHR)
     - Stock conc : 2.5 uM in nuclease-free water
     - Load vol   : 2.5 uL per reservoir
     - Working    : ~0.5 uM after merging with cleavage buffer (1:5 dilution)

  2. CLEAVAGE BUFFER (1.25x concentrated)
     - 125 mM NaCl
     - 62.5 mM Tris-HCl, pH 7.5
     - 31.25 mM KCl
     - 12.5 mM MgCl2
     - 0.0625% Tetronic 304 (surfactant to prevent biofouling)
     - 2.5 uM Quenched Probe (QP)
     - 6.25 uM Input ssDNA (IP) -- or as specified per experiment
     - Load vol   : 2 uL pipetted directly onto detection points

  3. QUENCHED PROBE (QP)
     - Composition : Two ssDNA strands (purchased from Alpha DNA, Montreal, Canada)
         F-strand  : 5' end labeled with Cy5 fluorophore; 0.625 uM
         Q-strand  : 3' end labeled with Black Hole Quencher-3 (BHQ-3); 0.7 uM
     - Prepared in 1.25x cleavage buffer (125 mM NaCl, 62.5 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5,
       31.25 mM KCl)
     - Annealing protocol (Biorad T100 thermocycler):
         Step 1 : 95 degC for 3 min  (denaturation)
         Step 2 : 50 degC for 15 min (annealing)
         Step 3 : 37 degC for 15 min (annealing)
     - Stored in bulk at -20 degC
     - Add 0.05% Tetronic 304 (per 10 uL) before use

  4. INPUT STRAND (IP)
     - Type     : Single-stranded DNA (ssDNA) activator
     - Function : Binds to ribozyme, inducing fold into active HHR, triggering self-cleavage
     - Conc     : 6.25 uM in cleavage buffer (standard); 0.01-10 uM for biosensor assays

  5. DI WATER
     - Used to fill 5 surrounding wells (30 uL each) on the 384-well plate
       to maintain humidity and prevent droplet evaporation during scanning

================================================================================
  RIBOZYME TRANSCRIPTION (Upstream Preparation)
================================================================================

  1. Design primers F1, F2, R1, R2 using the tool Primerize
     (https://primerize.stanford.edu)

  2. Assembly PCR (total volume: 100 uL):
     - Primers : F1 (2 uM), R1 (0.2 uM), F2 (0.2 uM), R2 (2 uM)
     - Enzyme  : HotStar Taq Plus (QIAGEN) with 1x reaction buffer
     - Additives: 1x Q-solution (QIAGEN), 0.2 mM dNTPs, milli-Q water
     - PCR program:
         95 degC x 15 min  (initial denaturation)
         15 cycles of:
           95 degC x 30 s  (denaturation)
           50 degC x 30 s  (annealing)
           72 degC x 30 s  (extension)
     - Ethanol precipitate the PCR product

  3. In vitro RNA synthesis (1 mL reaction):
     - Pool 10 x 100 uL PCRs, precipitate, resuspend in 150 uL milli-Q water
     - Reaction mix:
         80 mM HEPES, pH 7.5
         24 mM MgCl2
         40 mM dithiothreitol (DTT)
         2 mM spermidine
         6 ug/mL T7 RNA polymerase
         150 uL resuspended PCR product
         2 mM rNTPs
         1x pyrophosphatase (Roche Diagnostics)
         200 U (40 uL) RiboLock (Thermo Fisher Scientific)
     - Incubate 30 min at 37 degC
     - Add 10 U DNase (New England Biolabs); incubate 30 min at 37 degC
     - Extract RNA with phenol-chloroform; ethanol precipitate aqueous phase

  4. RNA purification:
     - Run on 10% denaturing polyacrylamide gel (8 M urea)
     - Visualize with UV-shadowing; excise highest band
     - Elute in 0.3 M NaCl overnight at 4 degC
     - Ethanol precipitate; resuspend in nuclease-free water

================================================================================
  DMF CHIP FABRICATION
================================================================================

  1. Design photomask in AutoCAD 2018; print on transparency film
     (CAD/Art services, Bandon, OR)

  2. Coat glass substrate with S1811 photoresist (Telic)

  3. UV expose for 8 s on Quintel Q-4000 mask aligner (Neutronix Quintel)

  4. Develop with MF321 developer (Rohm and Haas); bake 115 degC for 1 min

  5. Etch chromium layer with CR-4 chromium etchant (OM Group)

  6. Strip remaining photoresist with AZ-300T stripper (2 min)

  7. Rinse with DI water, air dry; silanize (DI water : 2-propanol :
     (trimethoxysilyl)-propyl methacrylate = 50:50:1) for 15 min

  8. Cover electrode pads with Kapton tape

  9. Deposit dielectric: 7 um Parylene-C via SCS Labcoter 2 PDS 2010

  10. Cut top plate from ITO-precoated glass slide
      (cat. no. CG-61IN-S207, Delta Technologies)

  11. Deposit hydrophobic layer: 1% Teflon-AF (FC40) on both plates
      - Spin coat: 500 rpm / 100 rpm/s / 30 s, then 3000 rpm / 500 rpm/s / 60 s
      - Bake: 160 degC for 10 min

  12. Assemble device: sandwich two layers of double-sided tape (3M Canada,
      70 um each) between bottom substrate and top plate

================================================================================
  AUTOMATION SYSTEM SETUP
================================================================================

  - Software         : In-house Python 2.7 scripts
  - Microcontroller  : Arduino Uno (Adafruit)
  - Signal generator : Agilent Technologies (15 kHz sine wave)
  - Amplifier        : PZD-700A (Trek Inc.) -- amplifies to 178 V RMS
  - I/O expander     : Maxim 7300 (Digikey), I2C communication
  - Interface        : Pogo pins connecting control board to chip contact pads
  - Top plate grounded via ground wire

  Protocol files specify sequential electrode actuation parameters
  (pulse time, period) and are run via command line.

================================================================================
  PROTOCOL STEPS
================================================================================

  STEP 1 -- CHIP CLEANING
  ------------------------
  1. Clean DMF substrate with RNAse Away, then IPA, then DI water
  2. Air-dry thoroughly before use

  STEP 2 -- REAGENT LOADING
  --------------------------
  1. Mount cleaned chip onto the automation system
  2. Pipette 2.5 uL of ribozyme solution (2.5 uM) into each ribozyme reservoir
  3. Pipette 2 uL of cleavage buffer (1.25x, containing IP and QP) directly
     onto each detection point
  4. Place the ITO top plate onto the chip and connect to ground wire

  STEP 3 -- DISPENSE RIBOZYME DROPLETS
  --------------------------------------
  1. Actuate dispense electrode pattern to stretch reservoir droplet
  2. Switch off the third electrode to pinch off a ~0.5 uL ribozyme droplet
     (actuation: 178 V RMS, 15 kHz, 0.7 s per step)
  3. Repeat for all assay lanes (rxn1, rxn2, rxn3) and for positive control (PC)
  4. For negative control (NC): dispense 2 uL quenched probe only (no ribozyme,
     no input strand) onto the NC detection point

  STEP 4 -- MOVE AND MERGE DROPLETS
  -----------------------------------
  1. Actuate electrodes sequentially to move each ribozyme droplet toward
     its corresponding detection point
  2. Merge the ribozyme droplet (~0.5 uL) with the pre-pipetted cleavage
     buffer droplet (2 uL) at the detection point
  3. Mix the merged droplet by actuating 4 electrodes back-and-forth for 10 s

     Reaction assignments:
       rxn1  : ribozyme droplet 1 + cleavage buffer 1  (assay replicate 1)
       rxn2  : ribozyme droplet 2 + cleavage buffer 2  (assay replicate 2)
       rxn3  : ribozyme droplet 3 + cleavage buffer 3  (assay replicate 3)
       pcMix : ribozyme droplet PC + PC buffer          (positive control)
       ncMix : quenched probe only                      (negative control)

     Positive control buffer contains ssDNA equivalent to the expected
     cleaved-off output RNA strand, directly displacing the quencher
     without requiring ribozyme activity.

  STEP 5 -- INCUBATION
  ---------------------
  Upon merging, in the assay droplets:
    - The input strand (IP) binds to the ribozyme
    - HHR folds into its active form and self-cleaves
    - The cleaved-off RNA output strand hybridizes to the F-strand of the
      quenched probe, displacing the BHQ-3 Q-strand (TMSDR)
    - Cy5 fluorophore is unquenched and emits fluorescence

  Temperature : 37 degC (maintained by plate reader during scanning)
  Duration    : 180 min total

  STEP 6 -- FLUORESCENCE DETECTION SETUP
  ----------------------------------------
  1. Carefully remove chip from automation system
  2. Stack chip onto a Corning 384-well plate (black-sided, flat clear-bottom)
  3. Align all detection points precisely to individual wells
  4. Fill 5 surrounding wells (around the chip perimeter) with 30 uL DI water
     each to prevent droplet evaporation
  5. Cover well-plate with lid and seal with parafilm
  6. Insert into ClarioSTAR plate reader (BMG Labtech)

  Plate Reader Settings:
    Excitation wavelength  : 647 nm
    Emission wavelength    : 665 nm
    Temperature            : 37 degC
    Flashes per well       : 8
    Focal height           : 15.80 mm
    Gain                   : 1800
    Scan matrix            : 30 x 30 pixels (10 mm2 per pixel)
    Scan interval          : every 20 min
    Total monitoring time  : 180 min (10 readings)

  Note: Calibrate focal length and gain using 10 uL of 0.5 uM Cy5 fluorophore
  (focal length = 6.4 mm for well-plate reference; chip focal height = 15.80 mm)

  STEP 7 -- REAL-TIME FLUORESCENCE READINGS
  -------------------------------------------
  Take well-scans at the following time points:

    Reading  1 : t =   0 min  (baseline)
    Reading  2 : t =  20 min
    Reading  3 : t =  40 min
    Reading  4 : t =  60 min
    Reading  5 : t =  80 min
    Reading  6 : t = 100 min
    Reading  7 : t = 120 min
    Reading  8 : t = 140 min
    Reading  9 : t = 160 min
    Reading 10 : t = 180 min  (endpoint)

  From the scanned pixel matrix, select the region corresponding to each
  detection point and record the highest fluorescence intensity value.

  Note: Do not use filler oil to prevent evaporation -- the probe can leak
  into the oil causing cross-contamination. DI water in surrounding wells
  is the preferred approach.

  STEP 8 -- ENDPOINT EVALUATION
  -------------------------------
  At t = 180 min, compare fluorescence intensities against expected values:

    Positive control (PC) : ~12,250 fluorescence units  (strand displacement)
    Assay ribozyme (RZ)   :  ~4,900 fluorescence units  (active HHR cleavage)
    Negative control (NC) :  ~1,530 fluorescence units  (quenched probe only)

  Threshold for positive cleavage signal: >= 2,000 fluorescence units

  Dispose reaction droplets from chip after reading is complete.

================================================================================
  BIOSENSOR ASSAY (OPTIONAL -- CONCENTRATION-DEPENDENT MEASUREMENTS)
================================================================================

  To use the ribozyme as a biosensor for quantifying input ssDNA concentration,
  perform the cleavage assay at multiple input concentrations:

    Test concentrations (uM): 0.01, 0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 5.0, 10.0
    Include a no-input control for each run.

  Read fluorescence every 20 min for 180 min (same as standard assay).

  Standard Curve Generation:
    1. Plot endpoint (t = 180 min) fluorescence vs. log10(input concentration)
    2. Fit a four-parameter logistic (4-PL) sigmoidal model to the full curve
    3. Extract the near-linear region (0.1 to 1.0 uM) for linear regression
    4. Use the slope of the linear fit as the sensitivity of the biosensor

  Biosensor Performance (DMF chip vs. well-plate):
    Sensitivity slope  : DMF = 1230 +/- 71     |  Well-plate = 850 +/- 120
    Limit of Detection : DMF = 1475 units       |  Well-plate = 1845 units
    Limit of Quant.    : DMF = 0.025 uM         |  Well-plate = 0.31 uM
    SNR (at 180 min)   : DMF = 18.95            |  Well-plate = 8.95

  LoD  = blank_mean + 3 x SD
  LoQ  = 10 x SD / S
  (where SD = std dev of no-input blank; S = slope of linear regression)

================================================================================
  KINETICS ANALYSIS
================================================================================

  To extract cleavage rate constants:
    1. Calculate inverse of fluorescence intensities at each time point
       (1/F represents amount of uncleaved ribozyme remaining)
    2. Fit a one-phase decay equation to the data:

         Y = (Y0 - Plateau) x e^(-K x X) + Plateau

       where:
         Y         = amount of uncleaved ribozyme (1/fluorescence)
         X         = time (min)
         K         = observed cleavage rate constant (kobs, min-1)
         Y0        = initial value of Y
         Plateau   = Y value at reaction completion

  Measured rate constants:
    kobs (DMF chip)   : 0.026 +/- 0.005 min-1   (R2 = 0.95)
    kobs (well-plate) : 0.010 +/- 0.003 min-1   (R2 = 0.84)

  Note: DMF cleavage rate is ~2.5x faster than well-plate, attributed to the
  higher surface-area-to-volume (SAV) ratio of droplets on the DMF platform
  and possible molecular crowding effects from Tetronic 304 surfactant.

================================================================================
  DATA ANALYSIS
================================================================================

  - Fluorescence data analysis : Prism 8 (GraphPad Software)
  - Droplet image analysis     : ImageJ 1.52p (Fiji)
  - Linear regression for kinetics and biosensor curves performed in Prism 8
  - SNR calculated as: mean fluorescence of triplicates / standard deviation

================================================================================
  WELL-PLATE REFERENCE ASSAY (BENCHTOP COMPARISON)
================================================================================

  For well-plate parallel experiments (384-well plate, black-sided, clear-bottom):
    1. Add 2 uL ribozyme (2.5 uM) to each well
    2. Add 8 uL cleavage buffer (1.25x) containing 6.25 uM input ssDNA
       and 12.5 mM MgCl2
    3. Fill 5 adjacent wells with 30 uL water each (humidity control)
    4. Cover with lid, seal with parafilm
    5. Insert into ClarioSTAR plate reader
    6. Read every 20 min for 180 min at 37 degC
       (8 flashes; lex = 647 nm; lem = 665 nm; focal length = 6.4 mm; gain = 1800)

================================================================================
  CONTROLS SUMMARY
================================================================================

  Positive Control (PC):
    - Cleavage buffer containing ssDNA identical in sequence to the expected
      cleaved-off RNA output strand
    - Directly displaces the BHQ-3 Q-strand from the probe via TMSDR
    - Confirms probe and detection system are functioning correctly
    - Expected fluorescence at 180 min: ~12,250 units

  Negative Control (NC):
    - Quenched probe only; no ribozyme, no input strand, no MgCl2 input
    - Probe remains fully quenched throughout the experiment
    - Represents baseline/background fluorescence
    - Expected fluorescence at 180 min: ~1,530 units

  Additional negative controls (second chip experiment):
    NC1 : Quenched probe in cleavage buffer (no input, no MgCl2 activation)
    NC2 : Buffer without MgCl2 and without input strand
    NC3 : Buffer with MgCl2 only (no input strand)
    NC4 : Buffer with input strand only (no MgCl2)
    NC5 : Buffer with mutated input ssDNA and MgCl2 (non-cognate activator)

================================================================================
  IMPORTANT NOTES
================================================================================

  1. SURFACTANT: Add 0.05% Tetronic 304 to all aqueous droplets to prevent
     biofouling and improve droplet movement on the hydrophobic chip surface.
     High-salt solutions (cleavage buffer) are particularly prone to sticking.

  2. EVAPORATION: Do not use filler oil; use DI water in surrounding wells
     instead. Oil causes the Cy5 probe to leak out and cross-contaminate
     adjacent detection points.

  3. CHIP REUSE: Pipette cleavage buffer directly onto detection points
     (rather than moving it across the chip) to prevent trace contamination
     of electrodes and enable chip reuse.

  4. READING INTERVAL: The ClarioSTAR takes up to 20 min to scan a full chip.
     Therefore, fluorescence readings represent 20 min interval snapshots,
     not instantaneous time points.

  5. ELECTRODE ACTUATION: Electrode pulse duration = 0.7 s per step.
     Dispense pattern: stretch droplet with 4 electrodes, switch off 3rd
     electrode to pinch off the smaller droplet.

  6. RNA HANDLING: Use RNAse-free reagents and surfaces throughout. Clean
     chip with RNAse Away before each experiment.
