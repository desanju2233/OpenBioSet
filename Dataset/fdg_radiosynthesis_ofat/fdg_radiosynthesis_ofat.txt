================================================================================
PROTOCOL: [18F]FDG Radiosynthesis on Digital Microfluidics (OFAT-Optimized)
================================================================================

Source Link : https://www.shihmicrolab.ca/_files/ugd/f0a828_ab1a29cf1f1a4c74999bfd94b207f38b.pdf
Paper DOI   : https://doi.org/10.1039/d2lc00764a
BioScript   : fdg_radiosynthesis_ofat.bs
Platform    : Digital Microfluidics (EWOD / DMFB electrode-array device)
Optimization: One-Factor-At-A-Time (OFAT) + Machine Learning (Ridge Regression)
Product     : [18F]FDG (2-[18F]fluoro-2-deoxy-D-glucose)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OFAT-OPTIMAL PARAMETERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Parameter                          Optimal Value
  ---------------------------------  ----------------
  Mannose triflate / [18F] volume    1.0 (equal ratio)
  Mannose triflate concentration     30 mM
  Radiolabeling temperature          100 C
  Radiolabeling time                 5 min
  NaOH concentration                 1.0 M
  Deprotection temperature           50 C
  Deprotection time                  5 min

  Fluorination efficiency (OFAT)   : 70.82 +/- 1.54%
  Overall synthesis yield (OFAT)   : 66.64%
  Overall synthesis yield (ML)     : 79.13%
  Total synthesis time             : ~19 +/- 2 min
  Post-purification purity         : ~93.05 +/- 2.46%

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REAGENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. [18F]Fluoride         - No-carrier-added [18F]fluoride ion in [18O]-enriched H2O
  2. Mannose Triflate      - 30 mM in anhydrous acetonitrile (MeCN); substrate for
                             [18F] fluorination
  3. Kryptofix K222        - K222 / K2CO3 complex; activates [18F]fluoride for
                             nucleophilic substitution
  4. NaOH                  - 1.0 M sodium hydroxide; deprotection agent

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEVICE SETUP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - DMF bottom plate: chromium-coated glass, standard photolithographic fabrication
  - Dielectric layer : parylene-C (7 um)
  - Hydrophobic layer: Teflon AF 1600 (150 nm), spin-coated at 1500 rpm
  - Top plate        : ITO-coated glass, Teflon AF 1600 coated
  - Inter-plate gap  : ~140 um (2x double-sided tape spacer)
  - Actuation        : 160 V_RMS sine wave at 15 kHz; 104 pogo-pin holder
  - Heating/cooling  : 14.8 x 14.8 mm TEC/Peltier module (10 central electrodes
                       defined as reaction site)
  - Reagent delivery : stepper-motor-driven syringe system
  - Purification site: alumina disc (6 mm dia., neutral alumina 50-300 um in PDMS)
                       sandwiched between top and bottom plates

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROTOCOL STEPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 - Deliver [18F]Fluoride
  - Dispense no-carrier-added [18F]fluoride to the reaction site via syringe.

STEP 2 - Activate [18F] (Carrier Evaporation)
  - Heat [18F]fluoride droplet at 120 C for 10 min.
  - Evaporates [18O]-H2O carrier; TEC/Peltier heater used.

STEP 3 - Resuspend with Kryptofix K222 / K2CO3
  - Dispense Kryptofix K222 droplet and mix with activated [18F] for 5 s.
  - Perform MeCN azeotropic dry: heat at 120 C for 5 min to remove residual water.

STEP 4 - Merge Mannose Triflate with Activated [18F]
  - Dispense 30 mM mannose triflate droplet (volume ratio 1:1 with [18F]).
  - Mix with activated [18F] droplet for 10 s to form reaction droplet.

STEP 5 - Fluorination (Radiolabeling)
  - Heat reaction droplet at 100 C for 5 min.
  - Nucleophilic substitution incorporates [18F] into mannose triflate scaffold.

STEP 6 - Deprotection (NaOH Hydrolysis)
  - Dispense 1.0 M NaOH droplet; mix with reaction droplet for 5 s.
  - Heat at 50 C for 5 min.
  - Hydrolyses the intermediate to yield crude [18F]FDG at room temperature.

STEP 7 - On-Chip Alumina Disc Purification
  - Actuate crude [18F]FDG droplet to the purification site (6 mm alumina disc).
  - Incubate at 25 C for 40 min; move droplet around adjacent electrodes to
    enhance contact with disc.
  - Removes Kryptofix K222, unreacted [18F]fluoride, and free F-.
  - Expected purity: ~93.05 +/- 2.46%.
  - After incubation, actuate purified droplet away from disc; collect manually
    by pipette for downstream processing.

STEP 8 - Detect Fluorination Efficiency (Quality Gate)
  - Analyse purified product by radioactive TLC (silica gel HLF plates).
  - Detection time: 30 s.
  - Quality threshold: fluorination efficiency >= 70%.
    PASS (>= 70%) -> drain / collect product for use.
    FAIL (< 70%)  -> do not collect; dispose.

STEP 9 - Waste Disposal
  - Dispose all intermediate waste droplets:
    [18F]fluoride residual, K222 droplet, mannose triflate residual, NaOH droplet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DETECTION & ANALYSIS INSTRUMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Dose calibrator         : CRC 55 TR (Capintec) - radioactivity measurement
  - TLC scanner             : MiniGITA star (Raytest) - fluorination efficiency
  - TLC plates              : Silica Gel HLF 250 um, 10x20 cm scored (Analtech)
  - Gamma spectrometer      : in-house; identity and purity of [18F]fluoride

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - The OFAT method requires only 8 experiments (1 base + 7 variable) compared
    to 2187 experiments needed for a full 3^7 factorial design.
  - Significant parameters identified by OFAT (p < 0.05, t-test):
      mannose triflate concentration, NaOH concentration, radiolabeling
      temperature, deprotection time, mannose triflate / [18F] volume ratio.
  - Non-significant parameters: radiolabeling time, deprotection temperature.
  - ML model (Ridge regression, 55-experiment database, 80/20 train/test split,
    10-fold cross-validation) further improved overall yield to 79.13%.
  - Conditions predicted for 100% incorporation (110 C, 1.4 M NaOH, 90 mM
    mannose triflate) were not practically achievable on-chip due to poor droplet
    movement fidelity at extreme concentrations and temperatures.
