Adaptation note: The cited Neogen kit is a one-step direct competitive ELISA for fentanyl in urine. The original wet-lab protocol uses a single 45-minute room-temperature incubation for the conjugate competition, followed by a 3x manual wash cycle, and a 30-minute TMB substrate incubation. The .bs file diverges from this in ways consistent with adapting a bench protocol to a Digital Microfluidic Biochip (DMFB): (1) it explicitly breaks the initial dispense into sequential atomic operations (dispensing sample + antigen, then dispensing conjugate); (2) it specifies a 60-minute conjugate incubation rather than 45 minutes; and (3) it extends the wash loop to 6 cycles, using a discrete droplet routing method where 350 units of water are mixed with the stationary capture bead for 45 seconds per cycle. The protocol below is authored to describe the literal compiled operations of the .bs file, using the cited Neogen kit as the closest available real-world reference for reagent identity, competitive-immunoassay logic, and volume conventions—not as a claim of exact step-by-step provenance.

Protocol details:

This is a competitive enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) for screening fentanyl in urine.

Sample Preparation: Add 20 units of the urine sample and 1 unit of immobilized antigen (capture bead) to the reaction zone. Mix briefly to combine.

Conjugate Incubation: Add 100 units of fentanyl conjugate to the sample mixture. Mix the solution for 60 seconds to allow competitive binding between free fentanyl in the sample and the conjugate.

Incubate the mixture at 23°C (room temperature) for 60 minutes to reach equilibrium. Remove and discard the supernatant.

Wash Cycle: Wash the reaction zone six times to remove unbound conjugate. For each wash cycle, add 350 units of distilled water, mix for 45 seconds, and discard the wash supernatant.

Substrate Development: Add 100 units of TMB substrate to the retained bead. Mix the solution and incubate at 23°C for 30 minutes to allow the colorimetric reaction to develop.

Stop Reaction: Add 100 units of acid stop reagent. Mix with the developed substrate for 60 seconds to halt the enzymatic reaction.

Detection: Measure the resulting signal (fluorescence) over 30 minutes to obtain the negative/positive reading. Discard the final reaction mixture.
