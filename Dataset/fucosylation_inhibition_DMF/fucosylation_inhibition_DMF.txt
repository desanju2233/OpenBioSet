================================================================================
  PROTOCOL: FUCOSYLTRANSFERASE INHIBITION ASSAY USING IMAGE-ANALYSIS
             AND DIGITAL MICROFLUIDICS (DMF)
================================================================================

  Source Link : https://www.shihmicrolab.ca/_files/ugd/f0a828_fb291b4dee6147cd9c47c11d6308710c.pdf
  Paper DOI   : https://doi.org/10.1063/1.5088517
  Journal     : Biomicrofluidics 13, 034106 (2019)

================================================================================
  OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This protocol describes a fluorescence-based inhibition assay for
H. pylori alpha(1,3)-fucosyltransferase (FucT) performed on a digital
microfluidic (DMF) device. The assay uses the labeled synthetic disaccharide
4-methylumbelliferyl beta-N-acetyllactosaminide (MU-beta-LacNAc) as a
substrate. When FucT is active, it fucosylates MU-beta-LacNAc, preventing
its hydrolysis by glycoside hydrolases and resulting in low fluorescence.
When FucT is inhibited (e.g., by GDP), the substrate remains unfucosylated,
gets cleaved by BgaA and SpHex, and releases fluorescent 4-methylumbelliferone
(4-MU). Inhibition curves spanning four orders of magnitude are generated
on-device via serial dilution, with concentrations verified by an image-based
"pixel count" method.

================================================================================
  PLATFORM & DEVICE SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

  Platform        : Two-plate digital microfluidic (DMF) chip
  Chip dimensions : 3 x 2 in. (75 x 50 mm)
  Electrodes      : 93 actuation electrodes (avg. width ~1 mm) +
                    10 reservoir electrodes (4.1 x 3 mm^2 each) +
                    1 waste collection electrode
                    Total: 103 electrodes
  Electrode shape : "Skewed-wave" (sinusoidal intercalating, ~4.0 x 0.87 mm^2)
                    Minimum angles ~90 deg to prevent dielectric breakdown
  Electrode gap   : 30 um (interelectrode average)
  Plate gap       : ~140 um (two layers of double-sided tape)
  Top plate       : ITO-coated glass (Rs = 15-25 ohm)
  Bottom plate    : Chromium electrode-bearing glass with FluoroPel PFC1601V
                    hydrophobic coating
  Driving voltage : 120-160 VRMS at 15 kHz
  Automation      : Arduino Uno + PCB relay board + PaseMaker software
                    (Python 2.7, LLGUI.exe interface)
                    GitHub: https://github.com/shihmicrolab/Automation
  Imaging         : 3.0 MP CMOS USB camera (EO-3112C, Edmund Optics) +
                    10x C-mount close focus zoom lens (54363, Edmund Optics)
  Unit droplet    : ~500 nL

================================================================================
  REAGENTS & SOLUTION PREPARATION
================================================================================

  All solutions contain 0.05% Pluronics F-68 to prevent protein biofouling.
  All solutions are covered with a thin layer of silicone oil (applied by
  touching the droplet surface with an oil-coated pipette tip).

  1. HEPES BUFFER
     - 25 mM HEPES, pH 7.0
     - 0.05% Pluronics F-68
     - Use: diluent for serial dilutions and blank control

  2. GDP INHIBITOR STOCK
     - 80 mM GDP (guanosine 5'-diphosphate) in 25 mM HEPES pH 7.0
     - 0.05% Pluronics F-68
     - Use: inhibitor stock for serial dilution

  3. FucT ENZYME SOLUTION
     - 0.135 mg/mL H. pylori FucT stock
     - Working concentration: 0.03 mg/mL (0.6 nM) after dilution on-chip
     - Buffer: 25 mM HEPES pH 7.0, 0.05% Pluronics F-68
     - Note: Recombinant FucT expression follows previously described
       procedures (see supplementary material of source paper)

  4. GLYCOSYLATION MIX (reaction-initiating solution)
     Stock concentrations (2x, before on-chip dilution):
     - 40 mM MgCl2
     - 0.04 mM MU-beta-LacNAc
     - 0.08 mM GDP-fucose
     - 25 mM HEPES pH 7.0, 0.05% Pluronics F-68
     Final on-chip concentrations (after 1:2 dilution):
     - 10 mM MgCl2
     - 0.01 mM MU-beta-LacNAc
     - 0.02 mM GDP-fucose

  5. HYDROLYSIS MIX (reaction-stopping solution)
     Stock concentrations (5x, before on-chip dilution):
     - 125 mM EDTA
     - 0.25 mM BgaA (beta-galactosidase from S. pneumoniae, ~247 kDa)
     - 0.25 mM SpHex (N-acetylhexosaminidase from S. plicatus)
     - 25 mM HEPES pH 7.0, 0.05% Pluronics F-68
     Final on-chip concentrations (after 1:5 dilution):
     - 25 mM EDTA
     - 0.05 mM BgaA
     - 0.05 mM SpHex
     Note: BgaA and SpHex sequentially cleave unfucosylated MU-beta-LacNAc
     to release fluorescent 4-MU. Both enzymes must be present for signal.

  6. GLYCINE SOLUTION (optional, for bell-curve correction)
     - 1 M glycine, pH 10.4
     - Use: 5-fold dilution of final reaction droplets if a bell-shaped
       dose-response curve is observed (caused by GDP quenching of 4-MU)

================================================================================
  ASSAY PRINCIPLE
================================================================================

  UNINHIBITED (low/no GDP):
    MU-beta-LacNAc --[FucT + GDP-fucose]--> Fucosylated-MU-beta-LacNAc
    Fucosylated substrate is NOT cleaved by BgaA/SpHex --> LOW fluorescence

  INHIBITED (high GDP):
    GDP competes with GDP-fucose at FucT active site --> FucT blocked
    MU-beta-LacNAc remains unfucosylated
    Unfucosylated MU-beta-LacNAc --[BgaA then SpHex]--> 4-MU (fluorescent)
    --> HIGH fluorescence

  Fluorescence readout: lambda_ex = 365 nm, lambda_em = 460 nm

================================================================================
  ON-DEVICE PROTOCOL (42-STEP DMF PROCEDURE)
================================================================================

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEP 1 — Prime HEPES Buffer Reservoirs
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Manually pipette five droplets (~2.7 uL each) of 25 mM HEPES buffer
    onto five separate reservoirs on the DMF chip.
  - Place the ITO top plate on top of the device.
  - Apply silicone oil shell to each droplet using an oil-coated pipette tip.
  - These reservoirs serve as diluents for the serial dilution (Steps 3-10).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEP 2 — Load GDP Inhibitor Stock
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Pipette a ~3.1 uL droplet of 80 mM GDP solution to one of the five
    pipetting inlet electrodes (aligned to the edge of the ITO).
  - Apply a driving potential to the inlet electrode to pull the droplet
    under the ITO into the reservoir gap.
  - Apply silicone oil shell.
  - This minimizes time on-chip before use, reducing biofouling risk.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEPS 3-10 — Serial ~5.5-Fold Dilution of GDP (6 Concentrations)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Each dilution step:
    a. Dispense one unit droplet (~500 nL) of the current GDP solution.
    b. Move the unit droplet into the adjacent buffer-primed reservoir.
    c. Mix by pulling the merged volume along the linear path and actuating
       the reservoir electrode to pull it back (creates ~1:5 dilution).
    d. Dispense one unit droplet of the resulting dilution for the next step.

  Resulting GDP concentrations (calculated by pixel count method):
    Concentration 1: 36.0 +/- 4.25 mM
    Concentration 2:  6.2 +/- 0.40 mM
    Concentration 3:  1.1 +/- 0.01 mM
    Concentration 4:  0.22 +/- 0.01 mM
    Concentration 5:  0.04 +/- 0.006 mM
    Concentration 6:  0.006 +/- 0.001 mM

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEP 11 — Load Negative Control (Blank)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Pipette a ~3.1 uL droplet of 25 mM HEPES buffer (no GDP) to an
    empty reservoir via pipetting inlet (same method as Step 2).
  - This serves as the 0 mM GDP blank control.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEPS 12-19 — Prepare 1 uL Reaction Droplets for Each Concentration
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  For each of the 7 GDP concentrations (including blank):
    a. Dispense two unit droplets (~500 nL each) from the reservoir.
    b. Merge the two unit droplets into one ~1 uL droplet.
    c. Actuate the merged droplet away from the reservoir path.
    d. Actuate the remaining excess droplet in the reservoir to the waste
       electrode and wick away using a paper strip.
    e. Return the merged 1 uL droplet to its reservoir.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEPS 20-26 — Add FucT Enzyme to Each GDP Droplet
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Pipette a ~3.1 uL droplet of 0.135 mg/mL FucT stock to a reservoir
    inlet (as in Step 2).
  - For each of the 7 GDP concentrations:
    a. Dispense one unit droplet (~500 nL) of FucT from the reservoir.
    b. Merge and mix the FucT droplet with the 1 uL GDP droplet
       in its respective reservoir (mix time: 5 s).
  - Incubate for 5 minutes at room temperature.
    Purpose: GDP competitively binds to the FucT active site.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEPS 27-33 — Add Glycosylation Mix (Initiate Fucosylation Reaction)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - For each of the 7 reaction droplets:
    a. Dispense one unit droplet (~500 nL) of glycosylation mix.
    b. Merge and mix with the FucT + GDP droplet (mix time: 5 s).
  - Incubate for 10 minutes at room temperature.
    Purpose: FucT transfers fucose from GDP-fucose onto MU-beta-LacNAc.
    If GDP has inhibited FucT, MU-beta-LacNAc remains unfucosylated.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEPS 34-41 — Add Hydrolysis Mix (Stop Reaction / Reveal Signal)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - For each of the 7 reaction droplets:
    a. Dispense one unit droplet (~500 nL) of hydrolysis mix.
    b. Merge and mix with the reaction droplet (mix time: 5 s).
  - Incubate for 5 minutes at room temperature.
    Purpose: BgaA cleaves galactose, then SpHex cleaves N-acetylglucosamine
    from unfucosylated MU-beta-LacNAc, releasing fluorescent 4-MU.
    Fucosylated substrate is sterically blocked and yields no signal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEP 42 — Fluorescence Detection
------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Align and mount the DMF device onto a Nunc 384-well black polystyrene
    microplate.
  - Scan the device surface using a ClarioSTAR monochromator microplate
    reader (BMG Labtech):
      lambda_ex  : 360-365 nm
      lambda_em  : 450-460 nm
      Gain       : 750
      Focal height: 15.80 mm
      Flashes    : 40
      Scan step  : 30 x 30 pixel increments (each pixel = 10 mm^2)
  - Output: fluorescence heatmap of the entire chip surface.

================================================================================
  PIXEL COUNT (PC) — IMAGE-BASED CONCENTRATION METHOD
================================================================================

  The "pixel count" (PC) method calculates droplet volumes and inhibitor
  concentrations directly from on-device images, without requiring external
  detectors or plate readers at the dilution step.

  Procedure:
    1. Capture an image of the droplet using the on-board CMOS camera.
    2. Binarize the image (convert to 1s and 0s) to enhance droplet boundaries.
    3. Apply edge-finding algorithm to trace the droplet boundary.
    4. Calculate droplet area from the traced boundary.
    5. Calculate droplet volume:
         Volume = droplet area x 140 um (plate gap height)
    6. Apply C1V1 = C2V2 to calculate the concentration after each dilution.

  Validation: PC volumes agree more closely with micropipette readings than
  gravimetric (precision balance) measurements. Fluorescence deviation
  between PC-calculated and direct fluorescence measurements is <0.05%.
  Homogeneity of dispensed droplets from the same mixed source: <3% CV.

================================================================================
  OPTIONAL: GLYCINE DILUTION (Bell-Curve Correction)
================================================================================

  If the dose-response curve shows a bell-shaped (non-sigmoidal) profile
  at the highest GDP concentrations (~40 mM), this is likely due to
  GDP quenching of 4-MU fluorescence rather than a true inhibition effect.

  Correction procedure:
    - After the hydrolysis incubation (Step 41), dilute each reaction
      droplet 5-fold into 1 M glycine, pH 10.4.
    - This restores the sigmoidal curve shape and improves reproducibility
      by reducing solution viscosity.
    - Expected IC50 after glycine dilution:
        DMF device  : 0.093 +/- 0.037 mM GDP
        Well plate  : 0.114 +/- 0.086 mM GDP
        (Not significantly different: p = 0.53, alpha = 0.05)

================================================================================
  DATA ANALYSIS — IC50 CALCULATION
================================================================================

  Fluorescence values are blank-corrected (subtract buffer-only signal).

  GDP concentrations are determined using the pixel count method.

  4-MU concentrations are calculated using a linear calibration curve
  derived from 4-MU standards pipetted and measured on-device.

  Dose-response curve fit (least squares):
    y = b + ((a - b) / (1 + (x / c)^d))

    where:
      y = fluorescence signal
      x = GDP concentration (mM)
      a = maximum signal (top plateau)
      b = minimum signal (bottom plateau)
      c = IC50 value
      d = Hill coefficient

  Note: Exclude ~40 mM GDP data points if bell-shaped behavior is observed
  before glycine dilution, as they represent artefactual signal quenching.

================================================================================
  ASSAY PERFORMANCE METRICS
================================================================================

  Z-factor (well-plate validation)   : 0.78  (n = 8)
  Droplet dispensing variability     : ~9% CV
  On-chip fluorescence homogeneity   : <3% CV
  PC vs. fluorescence deviation      : <0.05%
  Orders of magnitude of dilution    : 4 (10,000-fold range)
  Silicone oil effect on 4-MU signal : Not significant (p = 0.143)

================================================================================
  CONTROLS
================================================================================

  Positive control (max signal):
    - 0 nM FucT + full hydrolysis mix
    - Expected: ~6 uM 4-MU released from 10 uM MU-beta-LacNAc

  Negative control (min signal):
    - 0.6 nM FucT, no hydrolysis mix
    - Expected: minimal fluorescence (~1 uM 4-MU baseline)

  Blank:
    - 0 mM GDP + 0.6 nM FucT + full glycosylation and hydrolysis mix
    - Expected: low fluorescence (FucT fully active, substrate fucosylated)

================================================================================
  CRITICAL NOTES
================================================================================

  1. BIOFOULING PREVENTION
     - Add 0.05% Pluronics F-68 to ALL solutions containing proteins
       (FucT, BgaA, SpHex).
     - Apply silicone oil shell to all droplets; this is the single most
       effective measure: it allows >24 consecutive droplet movements vs.
       failure within 4-8 movements without oil.
     - Use HEPES buffer (not Tris): Tris caused fouling after ~4 movements
       even with Pluronics; HEPES lasted ~8 movements. With oil + HEPES +
       Pluronics, >24 movements were achieved without slowing.
     - Load protein solutions via pipetting inlet electrodes and use
       immediately; avoid long dwell times on the reservoir surface.

  2. ELECTRODE DESIGN
     - Use "skewed-wave" shaped electrodes. Conventional square electrodes
       of equivalent area require 9-13 electrodes to span the same distance
       and require 27% larger minimum droplet volumes to avoid stranding.
     - Sinusoidal curves with minimum ~90 deg angles prevent dielectric
       breakdown (which occurs at >10^8 V/cm with pointed electrode shapes).

  3. DROPLET VOLUME CONSISTENCY
     - Consistent dispensing is critical for accurate inhibition curves.
     - Verify all droplet volumes using the pixel count method at each
       serial dilution step.

  4. 4-MU FLUORESCENCE SENSITIVITY
     - 4-MU fluorescence is sensitive to ionic conditions, pH, and water
       content. Prepare all 4-MU standards in the same buffer and surfactant
       conditions as the reaction samples.
     - The reaction components (buffer + Pluronics) may enhance 4-MU
       fluorescence above the theoretical maximum; account for this when
       interpreting absolute signal values.

  5. GDP QUENCHING ARTEFACT
     - At GDP concentrations of ~40 mM, a false drop in fluorescence signal
       may be observed. This is NOT inhibition reversal but rather
       concentration-dependent quenching of 4-MU by GDP.
     - Apply glycine dilution (see above) to correct this effect.
