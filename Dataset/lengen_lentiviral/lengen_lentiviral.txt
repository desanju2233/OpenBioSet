================================================================================
PROTOCOL: LENGEN - Lentiviral Generation, Packaging, and Transduction
         on a Digital Microfluidic (DMF) Platform
================================================================================

SOURCE LINK : https://40332f11-eff8-4959-967a-6c98c26063bd.filesusr.com/ugd/447feb_13f855ea4ef54fee80498ee6773b93b0.pdf
PAPER DOI   : https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.analchem.1c05227
PUBLISHED   : Analytical Chemistry, 2022, Vol. 94, pp. 4039-4047

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

LENGEN is the first microfluidic method that integrates lentiviral generation,
packaging, and transduction on a single digital microfluidic (DMF) platform.
It produces viral titers between 10^6 and 10^7 TU/mL (comparable to macroscale)
and achieves high transduction efficiency for hard-to-transfect cell lines.
The total protocol runs in approximately 2 days vs. ~2 weeks at macroscale,
with 100-fold volume savings and no ultracentrifugation or manual purification.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEVICE SPECIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Platform                  : Digital Microfluidic (DMF), two-plate system
  Bottom plate electrodes   : 80 per set (2 sets total = 160 electrodes)
  Production site           : 1.6 mm diameter hydrophilic spot (x1 per set)
  Transduction sites        : ~1.2 mm diameter hydrophilic spots (x6 per set)
  Multiplexing              : 12-plex simultaneous transduction analysis
  Production droplet volume : ~3 uL (split into ~1 uL unit droplets)
  Actuation voltage         : ~500 V total, 15 kHz
  Electrode design          : Overlapping "prong" electrodes bussed via 2 wired
                              connections (22 electrodes per connection)
  Highway track             : One-directional delivery to 6 transduction sites

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REAGENTS AND MATERIALS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  CELLS
  - HEK293T            : Producer/packaging cell line
  - Target cell lines  : MCF-7, T47DKB-Luc, H1299 (breast cancer / lung)

  PLASMIDS (Core - Steps 1-8)
  - pMDLg/pRRE         : 0.75 ug  | Packaging plasmid
  - pRSV-Rev           : 0.75 ug  | Packaging plasmid
  - pMD2.G             : 1.5 ug   | Envelope plasmid
  - Transfer plasmid   : 3.0 ug   | e.g. pLv-mCherry (~4 kb)

  PLASMIDS (Optional Applications)
  - MISSION shRNA      : TRCN00000003300 | ESR1 knockdown (Step 9)
  - pLentiCRISPR-mCherry-NeoRv2 (LCMNv2) : ~15 kb | ESR1 knockout (Step 10)

  TRANSFECTION REAGENTS
  - Lipofectamine 3000 : 12.5 uL
  - P3000 Reagent      : 12.0 uL
  - Opti-MEM           : Total volume 500 uL
  - Pluronics F-127    : 0.05% w/v (added to all droplets for DMF actuation)

  CULTURE AND PROCESSING
  - Complete medium    : DMEM + 10% heat-inactivated FBS
  - DMEM + Polybrene   : 8 ug/mL polybrene + 0.05% Pluronics F-127 (diluent)
  - Trypsin-EDTA       : 0.25% w/v (cell detachment)
  - PBS                : Wash buffer
  - Puromycin          : 1 ug/mL (selection, Step 9 only)

  DETECTION
  - Fluorescence microscope : Olympus IX73 inverted
    Excitation / Emission   : 585 nm / 608 nm (mCherry)
  - Hoechst 33342           : Nuclear stain (total cell count)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROTOCOL STEPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 - SEED HEK293T PRODUCER CELLS
--------------------------------------
  1a. Grow HEK293T cells in 100 mm Petri dishes, wash with PBS, trypsinize
      with 0.25% Trypsin-EDTA, and resuspend in complete medium.
  1b. Count cells and prepare suspension at ~2.5-3.0 x 10^6 cells/mL.
  1c. Supplement cell suspension with 0.05% w/v Pluronics F-127.
  1d. Pipette 1.5 uL of cell suspension onto the bottom plate at the edge
      of the top plate. Apply driving potentials (~500 V, 15 kHz) to
      dispense and actuate droplet across the 1.6 mm hydrophilic production
      site (passive dispensing). ~1.5 uL adheres to the site.
  1e. Remove excess liquid to a waste reservoir with a KimWipe.
  1f. Incubate at 37 C until cells reach 50-70% confluency (~2500 cells).
      Duration: 24h

  NOTE: Cells can also be seeded onto the ~1.2 mm transduction sites in
  parallel at densities of 1.0-1.5 x 10^6 cells/mL (Step 5 may be done
  concurrently with Steps 2-4).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 2 - PREPARE LIPOFECTAMINE-DNA MASTERMIX
---------------------------------------------
  2a. Prepare plasmid mix in Opti-MEM:
        - 0.75 ug pMDLg/pRRE
        - 0.75 ug pRSV-Rev
        - 1.5 ug  pMD2.G
        - 3.0 ug  transfer plasmid (pLv-mCherry or equivalent)
      Mix each addition for 5s on the DMF device using the four-electrode
      linear array.

  2b. Prepare lipofection reagent mix:
        - 12.5 uL Lipofectamine 3000
        - 12.0 uL P3000 Reagent
      Mix for 5s.

  2c. Combine plasmid mix with lipofection reagent mix. Mix for 5s.

  2d. Add Opti-MEM to reach a total volume of 500 uL. Mix for 5s.

  2e. Add 0.05% w/v Pluronics F-127 for DMF actuation compatibility.
      Mix for 5s.

  2f. Incubate at room temperature (25 C) for 20 min to allow
      DNA-liposomal complex formation.

  NOTE: For shRNA knockdown (Step 9), replace transfer plasmid with
  MISSION shRNA plasmid (TRCN00000003300). For CRISPR knockout (Step 10),
  replace with LCMNv2 all-in-one Cas9/sgRNA plasmid (~15 kb).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 3 - TRANSFECT HEK293T CELLS
----------------------------------
  3a. Actuate the lipofectamine mastermix droplet (1.5 uL) toward the
      seeded HEK293T cells at the production site.
  3b. Circulate the merged droplet continuously around the four-prong
      electrode production area for 1 min (3 circulations) to ensure
      uniform mixing and replicate the reverse transfection procedure.
  3c. The formulation (3 uL total) is mixed in a circular fashion by
      actuating adjacent electrodes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 4 - LENTIVIRAL PARTICLE PRODUCTION
-----------------------------------------
  4a. Incubate the transfected HEK293T cells at 37 C for 24h (or 48h for
      higher titer).
  4b. Lentiviral particles accumulate in the cell supernatant.

  Expected output:
    - 24h: ~1.56 TU/cell | Titer ~6.5 x 10^6 TU/mL
    - 48h: ~2.65 TU/cell | Titer ~1.33 x 10^7 TU/mL
    - Comparable to macroscale benchtop: ~2.80 TU/cell after 5 days

  NOTE: The 24h timepoint is sufficient for downstream gene silencing and
  editing assays. No ultracentrifugation or purification is required.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 5 - SEED TARGET CELLS
----------------------------
  5a. Prepare target cell suspension (MCF-7, T47DKB-Luc, or H1299) at
      1.0-1.5 x 10^6 cells/mL in complete medium + 0.05% Pluronics F-127.
  5b. Pipette 1.5 uL onto the bottom plate; actuate across the six ~1.2 mm
      hydrophilic transduction sites via highway track passive dispensing.
      ~1.0 uL adheres to each site.
  5c. Remove excess liquid to waste reservoir.
  5d. Incubate at 37 C for 24h before transduction.

  Cell viability on device:
    - MCF-7    : 83.7% (48h), 85.7% (72h)
    - T47DKB-Luc: 98.4% (48h), 88.6% (72h)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 6 - SERIAL DILUTION OF VIRAL SUPERNATANT
-----------------------------------------------
  6a. After production incubation (Step 4), generate four serial dilutions
      of the lentiviral supernatant using highway track electrode actuation
      (~98% actuation accuracy):

      Diluent: DMEM + 10% FBS + 8 ug/mL polybrene + 0.05% Pluronics F-127

      Dilution series:
        1:3  - Merge 1 uL DMEM diluent with 2 uL viral supernatant (5s mix)
        1:6  - Merge 1 uL DMEM diluent with 1 uL of 1:3 product  (5s mix)
        1:12 - Merge 1 uL DMEM diluent with 1 uL of 1:6 product  (5s mix)
        1:24 - Merge 1 uL DMEM diluent with 1 uL of 1:12 product (5s mix)

  6b. Two control spots are prepared:
        - Control 1: Complete medium + 8 ug/mL polybrene (no virus)
        - Control 2: Complete medium only (no polybrene, no virus)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 7 - TRANSDUCE TARGET CELLS
---------------------------------
  7a. Actuate each dilution (1 uL) to a separate target cell site via the
      highway track one-directional electrode path. Mix each merged droplet
      for 5s on-site.
  7b. Flip the DMF device upside down and incubate overnight at 37 C to
      maximize lentiviral contact with target cells.
      Duration: 24h (sufficient) or 48h (for improved efficiency)

  Transduction efficiency (T47DKB-Luc):
    - 48h post-transduction : ~43%
    - 96h post-transduction : ~60%

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 8 - MCHERRY FLUORESCENCE DETECTION AND VIRAL TITER CALCULATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
  8a. After incubation, image each transduction site using an Olympus IX73
      inverted fluorescence microscope.
        Excitation filter : 585 nm
        Emission filter   : 608 nm

  8b. Imaging pipeline (ImageJ):
        i.   Split Hoechst (nuclear) and mCherry channels
        ii.  Apply threshold to each channel independently
        iii. Count total Hoechst-stained nuclei (cell counter)
        iv.  Count mCherry-positive cells
        v.   Calculate: % Fluorescence = (mCherry+ cells / Hoechst nuclei) x 100

  8c. Calculate viral titer:
        TU/mL = (# cells transduced x % fluorescent x dilution factor)
                / transduction volume (mL)

  8d. Dispose wells where transduced cell signal >= 2500 cells.

  Expected results:
    - Optimal dilution    : 1:3
    - Titer range         : 6.5 x 10^6 to 1.33 x 10^7 TU/mL
    - Functional titer    : ~1 x 10^7 TU/mL (on-device, 24h or 48h)
    - Macroscale benchtop : ~1 x 10^7 TU/mL (for comparison)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 9 - shRNA KNOCKDOWN ASSAY [OPTIONAL]
------------------------------------------
  Target gene : ESR1 (Estrogen Receptor alpha)
  Plasmid     : MISSION shRNA (TRCN00000003300, nontargeting shRNA as control)

  9a. Repeat Steps 2-8 substituting the transfer plasmid with the MISSION
      shRNA plasmid targeting ESR1.
  9b. After 24h transduction, retrieve transduced cells from the device:
        i.   Bring device to biosafety cabinet and separate top from bottom plate.
        ii.  Pipette 5 uL PBS onto each microwell; aspirate with Pasteur pipette
             (leaves only adhered cells).
        iii. Add 2 uL Trypsin-EDTA (0.25% w/v); replace top plate; incubate
             2 min at 37 C with 5% CO2.
        iv.  Pool 2 microwells of the same dilution by resuspending each in
             10 uL complete medium; transfer to 100 uL complete medium in a
             96-well plate.
  9c. Next day, refresh medium with complete medium + 1 ug/mL puromycin.
  9d. After 7 days outgrowth, collect cell lysate for qRT-PCR analysis.
        Method          : delta-delta-Cq (DDCq)
        Reference gene  : ACTB (beta-actin)
        Target gene     : ESR1

  Expected mRNA transcript reduction (MCF-7 cells, LENGEN):
    Dilution 1:3  -> 89% knockdown
    Dilution 1:6  -> 79% knockdown
    Dilution 1:12 -> 48% knockdown
    Dilution 1:24 -> 18% knockdown

  NOTE: Results are consistent with well-plate (macroscale) controls at
  equivalent MOIs (MOI 5 = 89% knockdown in well plate).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 10 - CRISPR-Cas9 KNOCKOUT ASSAY [OPTIONAL]
-------------------------------------------------
  Target gene : ESR1 (Estrogen Receptor alpha)
  Plasmid     : pLentiCRISPR-mCherry-NeoRv2 (LCMNv2), ~15 kb all-in-one
                Cas9 + sgRNA (available on Addgene)

  10a. Repeat Steps 2-8 substituting the transfer plasmid with LCMNv2.
       Note: Larger payload (~15 kb vs ~4 kb) will reduce transduction
       efficiency; optimize MOI/dilution accordingly.
  10b. Retrieve transduced MCF-7 cells from device as described in Step 9b.
  10c. Pool 1:3 dilution transduced cells (~2800 cells) into a 96-well plate
       for outgrowth and single-clone isolation.
  10d. After 7 days post-transduction, collect cell lysate and perform
       genomic cleavage assay (T7 endonuclease or equivalent):
         Parental band  : 409 bp  (blue arrow on gel)
         Cleavage bands : 235 bp and 174 bp (red arrows on gel)
         % Cleavage calculated from band intensity ratios.

  10e. Confirm mCherry fluorescence to verify transduction before cleavage
       assay (mCherry co-expressed with Cas9 in LCMNv2).

  Expected knockout cleavage efficiency:
    LENGEN (1:3 dilution) : ~6.9%
    Well plate MOI 0.5    : ~5.8%
    Well plate MOI 1      : ~6.2%
    Well plate MOI 2      : ~6.4%
    Well plate MOI 5      : ~9.4%

  NOTE: For H1299 cells with integrated eGFP, gene cleavage efficiency
  of 16.1% was observed using the same LENGEN workflow.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PERFORMANCE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Protocol duration     : ~2 days  (vs. ~2 weeks macroscale)
  Volume savings        : 100-fold reduction vs. macroscale
  Viral titer           : 1 x 10^6 - 1 x 10^7 TU/mL
  TU per cell (24h)     : ~1.56   (macroscale 5-day: ~2.80)
  TU per cell (48h)     : ~2.65   (macroscale 5-day: ~2.80)
  Cells per target spot : ~1400
  Multiplexing          : 12-plex simultaneous on one device
  Ultracentrifugation   : Not required
  Manual purification   : Not required
  Automation            : Full - droplet actuation programmed on DMF

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NOTES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - All cell types must be pre-cultured in 100 mm Petri dishes, washed with
    PBS, trypsinized, counted, and diluted to the required density before
    loading onto the DMF device.
  - Cells are resuspended in complete medium (DMEM + 10% heat-inactivated FBS)
    with 0.05% Pluronics F-127 prior to all on-device operations.
  - The two-plate DMF system has a removable top substrate; cells cultured on
    the device can be retrieved by trypsinization and transferred to well plates
    for validation (RT-PCR, sequencing) and expansion.
  - The five-step droplet operation procedure is performed in ~5 min with
    several incubation periods (Step 2 complex formation: ~20 min;
    production incubation: overnight).
  - For gene editing applications, 24h post-transduction is sufficient to
    observe knockdown and knockout events.
  - Lentiviral vectors up to 15 kb in size can be packaged on this platform.
