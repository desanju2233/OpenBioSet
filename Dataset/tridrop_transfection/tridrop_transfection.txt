Protocol Details:

Title: A Tri-Droplet Liquid Structure for Highly Efficient Intracellular Delivery in Primary Mammalian Cells Using Digital Microfluidics (triDrop Electroporation)

Overview:
The triDrop protocol uses digital microfluidics (DMF) to electroporate mammalian cells with biological payloads (dextran, plasmids, mRNA, or Cas9 ribonucleoproteins) using a three-droplet liquid structure. Two outer "liquid electrode" droplets of high-conductivity buffer flank a central sample droplet of low-conductivity buffer containing cells and payload. Applying voltage to the outer droplets generates a focused, homogeneous electric field through the central droplet, enabling efficient electroporation while protecting cells from electrochemical damage and excessive current.

1. Reagents and Materials
- High conductivity buffer (liquid electrode): PBS with 0.05% Pluronics F-68, conductivity ~16 mS/cm (immortalized cells) or ~32 mS/cm custom buffer (primary T cells in RPMI)
- Low conductivity buffer (sample/electroporation buffer): Type T electroporation buffer with 0.05% Pluronics F-68, conductivity ~8 mS/cm
- Cell types: HEK293, HeLa, Jurkat cells, and primary human CD4+ T cells
- Payloads: FITC-tagged dextran (70, 250, 2000 kDa), eGFP plasmid (~5 kb), eGFP mRNA (~1 kb), Cas9 ribonucleoprotein (RNP) with gRNA
- DMF device: bottom plate with chromium driving/reservoir electrodes, dielectric (SU-8 5) and hydrophobic (Teflon-AF) coating; top plate with gold electroporation electrodes and grounding plane

2. Cell Preparation
- Resuspend cells in low-conductivity electroporation buffer containing the desired payload
- Target concentration: at least 2 x 10^7 cells/mL
- Cell number per reaction: 20,000-40,000 cells (low-cell-number workflow)
- For plasmid/mRNA experiments: resuspend at 4 x 10^7 cells/mL with payload at 1.275 pg/cell (plasmid) or 2 pg/cell (mRNA)
- For CRISPR experiments: form Cas9 RNP by combining sgRNA (100 pmol per 1 million cells) with Cas9 protein (50 pmol per 1 million cells), incubate 10 min at room temperature before adding to cells

3. Reservoir Loading
- Load three reservoirs on the DMF device:
  - Two outer reservoirs: high-conductivity buffer (6 microliters each)
  - Middle reservoir: cells + payload in low-conductivity buffer (6 microliters)
- Reservoirs filled by pipetting fluid onto the bottom plate edge and applying driving potentials to draw fluid in

4. Droplet Dispensing and Actuation
- Dispense three ~1 microliter droplets (one from each reservoir) via pulling/necking
- Actuate the cell-containing droplet to the center of the electroporation site
- Actuate the two high-conductivity droplets to the outer edges of the electroporation site
- Actuation achieved via AC potentials of 300-400 V RMS at 15 kHz

5. triDrop Formation (Merging)
- Merge the three droplets into a continuous tri-droplet chain by actuating the high-conductivity droplets toward the central sample droplet
- Mixing between droplets is limited to diffusion; three discrete regions remain distinguishable for over 30 seconds post-merge

6. Electroporation Pulse
- Immediately upon merging, apply high-voltage DC square-wave pulses to the gold electrodes in contact with the flanking droplets
- Standard conditions (immortalized cell lines, e.g., HEK293/HeLa): 3 pulses, 200 VDC, 10 ms duration; effective field range 0.5-0.63 kV/cm
- Jurkat/HeLa optimized range: 0.75-0.90 kV/cm
- Primary T cells: 3 pulses, 450 V, reduced pulse duration of 1-3 ms (shorter durations improve viability)
- Electroporation pulse circuit: 8-pin optocoupler, DC power source, Arduino Uno-controlled pulse generator (100-450 VDC, 1-10 ms duration range)
- Total electroporation process completes within 5 seconds of droplet merging
- Total time from reservoir loading to electroporation: ~3 minutes for three triDrop structures

7. Off-Chip Transfer
- Remove the top plate immediately after electroporation
- Pipette the total tri-droplet volume (~3 microliters) off-chip into a well plate pre-loaded with warmed culture media
- Maximum cell exposure time in electroporation buffer: 20 minutes (to preserve viability)
- HEK293/HeLa cells: recovered in flat-bottom 48-well plates
- Jurkat/primary T cells: recovered in U-bottom 96-well plates

8. Post-Electroporation Recovery and Culture
- Incubate cells for recovery for more than 24 hours at 37 C in a humidified chamber with 5% CO2
- Primary T cells monitored daily for up to 7 days post-electroporation; viability becomes comparable to non-electroporated controls by day 5
- For CRISPR knockout experiments, cells are cultured for 72-96 hours post-electroporation prior to analysis

9. Analysis and Validation
- Flow cytometry (e.g., BD FACS Melody) to measure:
  - Viability ratio (VR): ratio of viable (DAPI-negative) electroporated cells to non-electroporated control
  - Transfection efficiency (TE): percentage of fluorescent living cells above threshold relative to total living cells
  - Mean fluorescence intensity (MFI) fold-change: ratio of MFI in transfected cells to non-transfected control
  - Knockout efficiency: percentage of living cells below a fluorescence threshold (for gene knockout assays)
- Fluorescence microscopy (bright-field and fluorescent imaging) for morphology and expression confirmation
- For CRISPR knockout validation (e.g., beta-2-microglobulin or TRAC locus): stain with FITC-tagged antibody and analyze fluorescence shift via flow cytometry

10. Key Reported Outcomes
- Transfection efficiency: greater than 95% with greater than 95% viability for dextran delivery in immortalized cells (HEK293, HeLa, Jurkat)
- Plasmid delivery: TE 71% / VR 90% (HEK293); TE 60% / VR 99% (HeLa)
- mRNA delivery in Jurkat: TE 95%, VR 98%, MFI fold-change greater than 500, using only 2 pg mRNA/cell
- Primary CD4+ T cells: plasmid TE >38%, VR 81%; mRNA TE 89%, VR 90% (using high-conductivity culture media flanks)
- CRISPR knockout efficiency: up to 80% (beta2M, Jurkat); 70% (beta2M, primary T cells); average 30-67% (TRAC locus, 10-condition arrayed primary T-cell experiment using fewer than 1,000,000 total cells)
- Comparable performance to Neon, Lonza, and Celetrix benchtop electroporation systems, while requiring substantially fewer cells (20,000-40,000 vs. 200,000-1,000,000+)
